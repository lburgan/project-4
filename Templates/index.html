Titanic Simulator

What is Titanic Simulator?

This "simulator" is really a machine-learning powered algorithm designed to predict deaths from the 1912 sinking of the RMS Titanic.

Titanic simulator takes in your demographic information (historical or fictional!) and predicts with 79% accuracy whether you would perish aboard the Titanic in 1912.

How to Use Titanic Simulator:

In order to use Titanic Simulator, you can use real historical data from a famous figure or relative, or you can make up your own data! Here are some things to consider:

  • You must enter the data exactly, including capitalization and spelling (i.e. Queenstown not queenstown, M not male)
  • The ticket fare variable takes in 1912 GBP, to get an idea of ticket fares you can go here
  • "Queenstown" is modern day Cobh, Ireland

About Titanic Simulator

This simulator runs on a Random Forest that was trained on real passenger records from the RMS Titanic. 1000 estimators were used in this forest, and we attained 79% accuracy. A variety of different models and different optimization methods were attempted (such as PCA), however this model was our highest-performing overall.
